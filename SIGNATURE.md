# SIGNATURE — Eterna Primavera

## The one concept
**"La Eterna Primavera."** Guatemala's own nickname is *the Land of Eternal Spring* — and
that is literally this restaurant's name. The whole site is built around that single truth:
a Guatemalan table that never goes out of season. Heritage-lux, proud, "100% Guatemalteco"
worn as a crown.

## The signature moment
A **feature-spread heritage hero**: a deep oxblood-red (#9C1F2E) editorial frame with a
hand-woven *huipil* textile thread running the border, the wordmark set in Bespoke Serif,
and a slow ken-burns drift across a real plated Guatemalan dish. As you scroll, the masa /
tortilla story unfolds in a magazine-style feature spread (oversized serif numerals, a
single hero dish per band). The craft verb is **"a mano"** — handmade: tortillas palmeadas,
chuchitos wrapped, caldo simmered. The signature interaction is a quiet **steam/heat shimmer**
over the hero plate and a textile-thread divider that "weaves" in on scroll — restrained,
not a pile-up.

## Lane honored
- Accent: #9C1F2E (oxblood, the Guatemalan flag's depth + chile rojo)
- Display: Bespoke Serif (Fontshare) — the heritage-lux voice
- Layout: feature-spread hero, heritage frame
- Mood: heritage-lux

## Why it's not generic
No sombrero-taqueria cliché, no neon cantina, no fiesta clip-art. It reads as a dignified
Central-American comedor with editorial restraint: serif type, woven-textile color language,
real food photography, oxblood + masa-cream + jade palette. Specifically Guatemalan, not
pan-"Mexican".
